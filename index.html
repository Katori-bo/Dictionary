Least Used Words in English

  • Lypophrenia :characterized by a feeling of profound melancholy or sadness without a clear cause.
  • Griffonage : is a term that refers to careless or hasty handwriting, often characterized by illegibility or scribbled notes.
  • Sybaritic :is an adjective that describes a lifestyle characterized by luxury, pleasure, and indulgence in the finer things in life.
  • Phosphenes :Phosphenes are the visual sensations of light that are perceived without any light actually entering the eye.
  • Ratiocination :refers to the process of logical reasoning or the act of drawing conclusions from premises.
  • Ululation :refers to a long, wavering, high-pitched sound, often resembling a howl or a wail.
  • Accismus :is a rhetorical term that refers to a form of feigned indifference or refusal to something that one actually desires or wants.
  • Petrichor :is a term that describes the distinctive, pleasant smell that accompanies the first rain after a long period of dry weather.
  • Borborygmus :refers to the rumbling or gurgling sound produced by the movement of gas in the intestines, often associated with digestion.
  • Defenestration :is the act of throwing someone or something out of a window.
  • Coign :is a somewhat archaic term that refers to a corner or angle, particularly in the context of architecture.
  • Pareidolic :Relating to the tendency to perceive familiar patterns, especially faces, in random or ambiguous stimuli.
  • Imprecation :A spoken curse or invocation of evil, often used to express anger or a desire for harm to come to someone.
  • Ethnocentrism :The belief that one's own culture or ethnicity is superior to others, often leading to the evaluation of other cultures based on the standards of one's own.
  • Limerence :An intense, involuntary emotional state of romantic desire for another person, characterized by obsessive thoughts, strong attraction, and a desire for reciprocation.
  • Jeremiad :A long, mournful complaint or lamentation, often reflecting on societal issues or misfortunes, typically in a prophetic or admonitory tone.
  • Flahoolick :A term used to describe a person who is excessively extravagant or wasteful, often associated with frivolous spending and a lack of concern for practical matters.
  • Loquacious :Tending to talk a great deal; very talkative or chatty.
  • Persiflage :Light and slightly contemptuous mockery or banter; playful and teasing conversation.
  • Deliquescent :Tending to dissolve or melt away, especially in the presence of moisture; also refers to substances that absorb water from the air and become liquid.
